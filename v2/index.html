Why we exist

Less than 5 percent of young Syrians access higher education across Jordan, Lebanon, Turkey, Egypt and Syria, leaving 91,000 missing out on higher education.

This limits their potential to contribute and integrate in their host country. Moreover, they aren’t as qualified for reconstructing their country and making it as safe and stable a place when they return home.

Losing your home shouldn’t mean losing your ability to learn and make the world a better place.

How we work

Community

We design and develop programmes alongside refugee communities, and build partnerships with local civil society organisations (CSOs).

Technology

To reach marginalised refugee students we use a combination of digital content, connected learning systems and social media to deliver programmes.

Partnerships

We partner with education institutions to leverage high quality content and programmes to support refugees accessing university.


Find out more about how we work

Our vision

Utimatly, we want to create a network of CSOs using and contributing open models and resources alongside our work providing funding and brokering of academic partnerships.

This will help support local leadership and the adaptation of connected learning, whilst drawing on the experience and scale of the network.
