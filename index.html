Why we exist

Today, only 1 percent of eligible refugees have access to higher education, compared to 36 percent of global youth. In the Middle East, this means that 91,000 young people are missing out on higher education.

This limits their potential to become self reliant, integrate and contribute in their host communities and to help reconstruct their country to make it when they return home.

Losing your home shouldn’t mean losing your ability to learn and make the world a better place.

The student journey

We are looking at the end to end learning journey for refugees to develop programmes that help them through each step.

  1. Step 1 : Awareness

    Online content

    Inspirational, informative advice for refugees interested in higher education.

    Guidance workshops

    Higher education options and how to evaluate.

    Peer mentorship

    Peer mentorship on programme and subject experiences.

  1. Step 2 : Preparation

    Peer mentorship on application experience

    Peer mentorship on application experience.

    Workshops

    Plan and prepare scholarship applications.

    Training

    English language training; comprehensive

    Academic skills

    Management, autonomous study, extended writing.

    Finance

    Crowdfunding student sponsorship for tuition fees and living costs.

  1. Step 3 : Enrollment

    Online content

    Peer online content about challenges faced in adapting to university.

    Finance

    Crowdfunding student sponsorship for tuition fees and living costs.

How we work

Community

We use participatory methods to design to and deliver programmes alongside refugee communities, and build partnerships with local civil society organisations to lead delivery.

Technology

To reach marginalised refugee students we use a combination of digital content, connected learning systems and social media to deliver education and support programmes.

Partnerships

We partner with education institutions to leverage high quality content and programmes to support refugees accessing university.


Find out more about how we work

Our vision

We believe that local leadership by civil society organisations (CSOs) and refugee communities will allow for more efficient, dynamic and effective programmes.

Ultimately, we are working towards creating a network of CSOs using and contributing to a commons of open models and resources. to scale support for refugees accessing higher education.
